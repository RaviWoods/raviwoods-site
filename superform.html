Break to Make

November 2017

A sculptural chair, exploring ways of generating form unpredictably, through broken ceramic products.

Initially, materials were paired with processes, creating a set of small prototypes.

The chosen approach was to break and repair ceramic artifacts. Pottery was used in prototyping. However, for the scale of a chair, porcelain bathware was used.

Creating form through breaking objects is an unpredictable process. Unpredictability was also explored in the finish of the piece, using ink thrown onto wet paint.

A number of designs were trialled, with a focus on the aethestics and stability of the design.

To highlight the shape of the bathware shards, the ink finish was rejected in favour of a cleaner design. The final piece was 5 shards, glued using JB-Weld (an epoxy used in car repairs), and finished with a white bathroom sealant.
